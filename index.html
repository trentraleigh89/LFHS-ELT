Life Time Raises 2026 Guidance The Same Week Planet Fitness Cuts It

DemandUS-CAN-MEX · Health Clubs

Life Time raises 2026 guidance as premium-tier dues hit $230/month

May 5: Life Time reported Q1 revenue up 11.7 percent to $788.7M, net income up 15.8 percent to $88.1M. Average monthly dues reached $230, up 10.5 percent YoY. Average revenue per membership rose 10.2 percent to $930. The company raised full-year 2026 guidance: revenue to $3.32B-$3.35B and adjusted EBITDA to $925M-$940M. Shares rose 10.4 percent same day. Capex up 82 percent to $260M as 14 new club openings front-loaded. CEO Akradi called GLP-1s "a home run" for the industry.

Why it matters In the same week Planet Fitness cut value-tier guidance, the premium-tier operator raised guidance and accelerated capex. The K-shaped divergence between US health club tiers is now confirmed by Q1 reports from both ends. Equipment specification at the premium tier remains a durable demand pocket.

DemandUS-CAN-MEX · Health Clubs

Planet Fitness cuts 2026 guidance after Q1 member-growth shortfall

May 7: Planet Fitness reported Q1 revenue up 21.9 percent to $337.2M but cut full-year revenue growth guidance to ~7 percent from 9 percent and same-club sales to ~1 percent from 4-5 percent.
